There was no drama. For once, at Bridgewater, there was no drama at all, and that suited everyone inside the ground just fine.

Bobington Rovers beat Ashwick Borough 2-0 on Saturday afternoon with a performance of quiet professionalism that would have delighted Phillipa Corbett even if it will not make the highlight reels. Nadia Osei scored in the twenty-second minute with a finish of clinical precision — a low, left-footed shot inside the far post after a neat exchange with Kael Dunmore on the edge of the area — and Miles Harte added the second midway through the second half, tapping in from three yards after Dunmore's driven cross from the right evaded two Borough defenders.

That was enough. More than enough.

Rovers move to 36 points from 28 matches, ten clear of Millwall Athletic in the final relegation place. Survival is not yet mathematically certain, but it is as close to it as makes no difference. Borough, who created little of note and whose visit to Bridgewater had the weary quality of a team running out of arguments, remain fifteenth on 28 points.

## Harwick Again

The story of Rovers' second half of the season has been, in large part, the story of Theo Harwick. The twenty-year-old centre-back, drafted in when Orin Blackshaw was injured in February, has been extraordinary. On Saturday he won eight aerial duels, made two clearances off the line in the first half — neither of which troubled him unduly — and marshalled the back four with the quiet authority of a man twice his age.

Blackshaw, who watched from the directors' box for the second consecutive match, was seen smiling when Harwick headed away a Borough corner in the sixty-first minute. The veteran is expected to return to training next week and could feature against Port Caravel Wanderers on the 21st, but displacing Harwick now would require either courage or cruelty.

Corbett, asked after the match whether Blackshaw's return presents a selection headache, gave what has become her standard response to questions she considers premature: "We'll see."

## Harte's Moment

The afternoon's warmest reception was reserved for Harte's goal. The midfielder, who has started five consecutive matches since coming into the side against Caravel City, has been tidy and effective without quite finding the moment that announces a player to the crowd. This was it.

Dunmore, who grows more creative with every match, drove down the right channel in the sixty-eighth minute, beat his man with a drop of the shoulder, and sent a low, hard cross along the six-yard line. Two Borough defenders stretched for it and missed. Harte, arriving at the back post with the timing of a man who had been waiting for exactly this ball, tapped it in.

He celebrated with restrained delight — a clenched fist, a jog toward the touchline — and the 38,500 inside Bridgewater rose in approval. It was his first league goal of the season.

"He deserved it," Corbett said. "He's done everything we've asked."

## Looking Ahead

The fixture list eases from here. Port Caravel Wanderers at home next Saturday, then away to Dunmore Eagles, then home to Millhaven. If Rovers take care of business — and this side looks capable of it — they will be safe with matches to spare, and Corbett's contract negotiation will take on a rather different complexion.

That, however, is for another day. Saturday was about the job at hand, and the job was done.

*Bobington Rovers 2 (Osei 22', Harte 68'), Ashwick Borough 0. Bridgewater Stadium, att: 38,500. Ref: A. Marsh.*
