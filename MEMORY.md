## Geography

### Bobington
A large, cosmopolitan city. Districts and landmarks:
- **The Docklands** — Waterfront district, commercial shipping, working-class character. Includes Greystone Wharf and Port Sovereign.
- **Midtown** — Commercial heart of the city
- **Upper Fernwich** — Leafy, affluent residential area
- **Thornhill** — Residential district, politically conservative council representation
- **East Palisade** — Residential district, politically conservative council representation
- **Caldecott Square** — Major public square, planned tramway interchange hub
- **Bramblegate Market** — Historic market, also home to the Bramblegate Exchange (commodities trading)
- **Copperton Lane** — Residential street
- **Ashbury Lane** — Location of the Thessarine consulate; also a neighbourhood with gas lamps
- **Threadneedle Street** — Home to Rensler's coffee house

### Broader World
- **Greymoor Highlands** — Rural/highland region, source of granite and landscape painting inspiration
- **Dunbar's Reach** — Coastal village, birthplace of painter Isolde Hargrove
- **Millhaven** — Village outside Bobington where Hargrove lives
- **Ashford Bay** — Coastal location, subject of Hargrove's famous graduation painting
- **The Kaelmar Strait** — Strategically vital waterway between the Delvarian Empire and the Thessarine Confederation
- **Ashen Bluffs** — Location of the 1987 treaty dividing the Kaelmar Strait
- **Verlaine, Port Caravel, the Ashford Republic** — Other nations/cities studied for tramway design

### Nations
- **Delvarian Empire** — Major power, currently posturing militarily in the Kaelmar Strait. Has state-controlled newspapers. Naval power.
- **Thessarine Confederation** — Island nation, controls southern channel of Kaelmar Strait. Smaller navy. Major copper exporter. Has a consulate in Bobington.

## Government & Politics

- **Mayor Harriet Blackthorne** — Current mayor of Bobington. Supportive of the tramway expansion. Expected to present Freedom of the City to the Rovers.
- **Council Speaker Desmond Falk** — Presides over City Council
- **Councilwoman Ida Pryce** — Led the tramway campaign for 8 years. Her district includes the Docklands. Called for inspection review after the Greystone Wharf fire.
- **Councilman Aldric Voss** — Vocal opponent of tramway expansion, concerned about cost vs. schools and fire brigades

The City Council voted 7-4 to approve the Veridan Corridor Tramway Expansion.

## Infrastructure & Economy

- **Currency:** The florin
- **Veridan Corridor Tramway Expansion** — Approved by council. 14 miles of new track, Docklands to Upper Fernwich via Midtown. 12 new stations. Cost: 3.2 billion florins. Phase 1 (Docklands–Midtown) breaks ground autumn 2026, service in ~2 years. Full line to Upper Fernwich by spring 2030. 6,000 workers at peak. Greymoor granite for station facades. Chief Transit Engineer: Yara Okonkwo.
- **Bobington Merchants' Guild** — Business organisation, urged compensation fund for shopkeepers displaced by tramway construction
- **Bramblegate Exchange** — Commodities exchange. Copper futures up 8% due to Kaelmar Strait tensions.
- **Ashcroft Property Group** — Owner of the destroyed Mercer & Holt warehouse, had unpaid tax dispute with the city
- **Kaelmar Strait trade** — ~1/3 of Bobington's copper imports and ~half its eastern spice trade passes through the strait

## Recent Events (Chronological)

1. **Greystone Wharf Fire** (13 Feb 2026) — Three-alarm fire destroyed the Mercer & Holt warehouse (built 1882), a Victorian-era landmark. Four firefighters injured, none critically. Building had been vacant 18 months. Arson not ruled out. Fire Marshal Hale investigating.
2. **Merchants' Cup Final** (14 Feb 2026) — Rovers 2-1 Ironhall United (AET). Goals: Cole 34' (IRN), Blackshaw 72' (ROV), Dunmore 118' (ROV). Cup parade Tuesday.
3. **Hargrove Retrospective Opens** (14 Feb 2026) — "Light Through Glass" at Royal Bobington Gallery. 120+ works. Hargrove made rare public appearance.
4. **Tramway Expansion Approved** (15 Feb 2026) — Council votes 7-4. 14-mile Veridan Corridor route. Breaks ground autumn.
5. **Kaelmar Strait Tensions** (ongoing, reported 15 Feb 2026) — 11+ Delvarian warships observed. Thessarine recalling reservists. Copper futures up 8%. Diplomatic situation unresolved.
