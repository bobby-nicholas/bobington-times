# Economy & Commerce

## Currency
- **The florin** — Bobington's currency. Subdivided into centimes (50-centime tram fare mentioned).

## Key Economic Facts
- **Copper** — Major import via Kaelmar Strait (~1/3 of supply). Opened Mon 24 Feb at 889, rose to 891 by midday. Eased to 885 Tue 25 Feb. Fell to 878 Wed 26 Feb on envoy designation. Eased further to 872 Thu 27 Feb on venue confirmation. Fell to 851 by 6 Mar. Closed Fri 7 Mar at 847. Mon 9 Mar: fell to 838. **Tue 10 Mar: opened at 835** — lowest since 5 Feb (pre-crisis). 8th consecutive decline. ESI at 321 (7th consecutive decline). Varga: market now "pricing in a resolution." Estimated tramway copper cost overrun: ~510M florins at 889, 620M+ at 950 (upper analyst forecast). Funding gap: ~160M florins between available financing (~350M via bonds) and overrun. Okonkwo: cost of delay is 2-3M florins/week in idle costs. Merchants' Guild: total fleet costs now ~1.8M florins; 14 vessels rerouted via Cape of Sarenne.
- **Municipal bond yields** — Fell from 4.2% to 3.9% on Mon 9 Mar (strongest single-day rally in 5 years). Continental Rating Agency: "constructive development." Reduces annual debt service by ~8-10M florins over 20-year issuance. Bond prospectus being drafted for phased approach financing.
- **Eastern spice trade** — ~half passes through Kaelmar Strait. Eastern Spice Index (Bramblegate Exchange, 12-spice weighted basket, est. 2003) hit record 348 (Mon 24 Feb close), up 22% in 10 days. Previous record 327 (2018 dock strike). Velveroot exhausted. Black cardamon +18%. Dried saffron bark, golden peppervine, smoked coriander seed +10-20%. At least 7 restaurants pulled signature dishes. Merchants' Guild emergency session Thu 27 Feb at Guild Hall (stockpile management, emergency pricing, petition to Foreign Office). First emergency session since 2011 dockers' strike.
- **Shipping insurance** — Premiums doubled for Kaelmar-route cargo. Some underwriters declining new policies. Shipowners rerouting via Cape of Sarenne (+12-15 days).
- **Coffee at Rensler's** — 4 florins (up from 3.60) due to shipping cost increases
- **Greymoor copper** — Domestic supply from Greymoor Highlands, ~2,000 tonnes/year from small mines (Miners' Cooperative of Greymoor, chair: Nils Haversten). Fraction of tramway's ~12,000 tonne need. Scaling up would take 18-24 months and tens of millions in investment.

## Major Projects
- **Veridan Corridor Tramway Expansion** — 14 miles, Docklands to Upper Fernwich via Midtown. 12 stations. Cost: 3.2 billion florins (380M allocated for copper components at 740 fl/tonne baseline). Phase 1 breaks ground autumn 2026 (pending commission recommendation). Full completion spring 2030. 6,000 workers at peak. Greymoor granite for facades. Chief Engineer: Yara Okonkwo.
  - **19 Feb council session:** Okonkwo presented three options: (1) Proceed, absorb 430M+ overrun via bonds (~28M/yr for 20 yrs); (2) Redesign with aluminium substitution (~30% copper reduction, 18-month delay, inferior system); (3) Phase — build Phase 1 only, defer Phase 2 until prices stabilise (2-3 yrs). Cancellation would cost ~180M in termination penalties.
  - **Municipal Treasury:** Contingency reserve at 142M florins (partially drawn down for Bramblegate Market roof repairs). City can raise ~350M via bonds without downgrade; a 500M+ overrun disclosed before groundbreaking would likely trigger Continental Rating Agency downgrade review, raising borrowing costs 40-60 basis points. Deputy Treasurer: Annabel Whitford.
  - **Copper Review Commission** established to report by 5 Mar. First hearing held 24 Feb.

## Spice Crisis (updated 28 Feb)
- Eastern Spice Index hit 356 on Wed 26 Feb (record); eased to 349 Thu 27 Feb — first decline in 13 trading days, driven by Guild rationing announcement and diplomatic optimism
- Nazari imposing purchasing limits on 18 of 32 varieties; says rationing framework allows supply to existing restaurant clients through mid-March
- At least 9 of 12 surveyed restaurants have made significant menu changes
- The Ashen Grill replaced entire eastern menu; The Willow Table introduced "Greymoor preparation"
- Fontenoy (Verlaine's) estimates ~2 weeks of reserves remaining
- The Thirty-Mile Table fully booked for first time (accidental beneficiary)
- Two smaller merchants shuttered: Hamid Rassam (Ashbury Lane, dried bark/root, closed 26 Feb), Elia Thorne (Chandler's Row, herb/spice, closed Mon 24 Feb)
- **Guild emergency session (27 Feb, Thu):** Unanimous vote (43 members) for three resolutions: (1) Spice Crisis Committee of 4 (3 merchants + 1 Guild officer) to coordinate shared stockpile inventory (first meeting Mon, inventory by Wed); (2) Emergency pricing cap — no Guild member may charge more than 250% of pre-crisis price; (3) Formal petition to Foreign Office for spice trade inclusion in Kaelmar talks — 67 signatures (up from 52), delivered by Voss in person with Nazari. Foreign Office: concerns "will be considered within the appropriate diplomatic framework."
- **Spice Crisis Committee named (28 Feb, Sat):** Nazari (chair), Simeon Kade, Marta Engel (wholesale broker, Threadneedle Lane), Alistair Ferris (Guild trade secretary). First meeting Mon 3 Mar at Guild Hall. Stockpile audit Wed 5 Mar. Preliminary estimates: combined Guild reserves 8-10 weeks at rationed levels. Cap (250% of 10 Feb prices) enforcement begins Mon. Cap covers ~80% of commercial spice trade by volume (Guild members only). ESI closed Fri at 347 (3rd consecutive daily decline). Velveroot: ~40 lbs remaining citywide.

## Fernwick Ferry & Bridge Repair
- Emergency ferry approved 25 Feb. Contract: 940,000 florins to Ashwater River Services (Gwen Alderly). Route: Thornhill Reach to Bramblegate Steps. 15-min intervals, 6 AM–10 PM, ~200 passengers/crossing. Fare: 30 centimes (subsidised). Monthly pass: 12 florins. Vessels: *Thornhill Star* (220 pax, Captain Morris Aldgate) and *Bramblegate Belle* (backup).
- **Launched 6 Mar at 6:00 AM.** Maiden crossing: 193 passengers, 14 minutes. 340 monthly passes sold by Thursday. Day 1: 48 crossings, ~8,200 passengers. Peak-hour queues 20-30 min at Thornhill Reach. *Bramblegate Belle* not needed.
- **Day 4 (Mon 9 Mar):** ~7,600 passengers. Peak-hour queues 20-25 min.
- **Bridge repair tender opened 9 Mar at noon.** 3 firms attended: Hallam & Stroud, Brannock Cable Works (Edgeminster, domestic), Vandersteel Ironworks (Ashford Republic). Formal bids due in 4 weeks. Kinnear presided. Estimated cost: 55-65M florins, 8-12 months.
