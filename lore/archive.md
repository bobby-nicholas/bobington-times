### Spice Trade Crisis — Resolved March 2026
Five-week disruption to eastern spice trade caused by the Kaelmar Strait naval standoff. 16 vessels rerouted via Cape of Sarenne at total cost of ~2.3M florins. Insurance claims: ~580K florins. Eastern Spice Index peaked at 356 (26 Feb), returned to 298 (15 Mar, pre-crisis ~295). City spice reserves fell to critical levels (velveroot exhausted, 38 lbs citywide at lowest point) before resupply vessels arrived: *Aldara* (5 Mar, 120 lbs), *Fernleigh Cross* (6 Mar, 480 lbs), *Havenport* (13 Mar, 220 lbs), *Stellara* (15 Mar, 380 lbs). Reserves exceeded 1,400 lbs at resolution. Merchants' Guild imposed 250% pricing cap (2 Mar), established 4-member Spice Crisis Committee (Nazari chair, Kade, Engel, Ferris). Key figures: Haroun Nazari, Simeon Kade, Marta Engel, Clement Varga. Lasting effects: several restaurants kept crisis-born dishes (Hale's herb roast outsells spiced duck 2:1); Kessling's Thirty-Mile Table booked through April; insurance market thaw beginning.
Key lore files: lore/economy.md, lore/institutions.md

### Municipal Chamber Clock — Resolved 17 March 2026
Quirke & Bramley clock (installed 1873) stopped at 2:47 PM Wed 11 Mar after 153 years continuous operation. Front pivot bearing worn after ~4.7 billion rotations. Only Desmond Quirke (79, Millhaven, grandson of co-founder Aldous Quirke) understood the mechanism. Quirke arrived Fri 13 Mar, diagnosed the fault, returned Mon-Tue 17 Mar with watchmaker's lathe. Turned phosphor-bronze bushing (3 attempts), reamed bearing seat, pressed new bushing, reassembled escapement. Clock restarted 10:14 AM Tue 17 Mar. First chime 10:30. Silent for 6 days 19 hours (longest since 1947). Quirke wrote 14 pages of maintenance instructions, sealed in envelope at Millhaven workshop. Key lore files: lore/geography.md, lore/people.md

### Bellvue Theatre Fundraising — Resolved 19 March 2026
The Bellvue Theatre (Marchmont Street, est. 1897) needed 180,000 florins for fly tower repairs by 1 May 2026. Owner-director Augustin Fell staked everything on fundraising + staging *The Lamplighter's Oath* (his first play, opening 10 April). Key fundraising events: Marchmont Street Traders' Association door-to-door (4,200 fl), Arts Council emergency grant (35,000 fl), benefit night 15 Mar (47,400 fl, Glass 20K, audience pledges 12.4K). Target reached 19 Mar at 3:17 PM: 180,200 florins from 171 individual donations processed by Norah Fell (19, Augustin's daughter). Final push included Polytechnic staff/students (1,200 fl), DWA (1,000 fl, Seldon), *Thornhill Star* ferry crew (800 fl), Desmond Quirke (500 fl, "For old things that work"). Thurston Brothers of Caldwell awarded repair contract at 178,500 fl; work begins 2 May. Key figures: Augustin Fell, Norah Fell, Ruben Glass, Thomas Ashworth, Nessa Holloway, Mrs Prudence Alleyn. Key lore files: lore/arts-culture.md, lore/institutions.md

### Lady Maren Found — Resolved March 2026
Fishing vessel *Lady Maren* (Captain Dermot Shale, 58) went missing 9 Mar, found drifting 19 NM NE of harbour by coastguard cutter *Resolute* (Captain Anne Dalrymple) at 4:20 AM 11 Mar. All 4 crew alive. Crankshaft fractured, radio aerial broken in squall. Shale rigged sea anchor from fishing nets. Deckhand Tobias Renn sprained ankle. Towed to harbour 11:40 AM. Incident catalysed maritime safety push — Fishermen's Benevolent Association voted unanimously (47 vessel masters, 12 Mar) for mandatory position-reporting beacons on all vessels under 20m (~90 boats). Story continues as "Maritime Safety — Position-Reporting Beacons" thread.
Key lore files: lore/people.md, lore/institutions.md

## Kaelmar Strait — Transit Corridor Framework (RESOLVED 24 Mar 2026)
- **Crisis:** Delvarian naval buildup in Kaelmar Strait (Feb 2026). Commercial shipping disrupted. Copper prices spiked. Spice crisis. Insurance market frozen.
- **Resolution:** Transit Corridor Framework signed Tue 24 Mar at Foreign Office, Chancery Row. Sir Duncan Hale (Bobington) and Count Viktor Soren (Delvaria) signed at 11:14 AM. Undersecretary Helena Marchetti presided. Henrik Dahl (Ashford Republic) signed observer's protocol.
- **Framework:** 4 pillars — designated transit corridor, Joint Maritime Inspection Commission (3 Delvarian, 3 Thessarine, 1 Ashford observer), insurance framework (premiums capped 140% pre-crisis), 3-month trial with 6-week and 90-day reviews. Secretariat in Fenmouth.
- **Key moment:** Soren signed with his father Admiral Pehr Soren's silver pen (Treaty of Ashen Bluffs, 1987).
- **Market reaction:** Copper at 741 (19th consecutive decline). ESI at 268. 12/14 insurance firms resumed underwriting.
- **Kharstad Gazette** ran it on front page for the first time.
- **Thornbury:** "A triumph of patience."
- **Key characters:** Hale, Soren, Marchetti, Dahl, Lindqvist, Miren, Thornbury, Tremayne.
- **First commercial transit** expected mid-April.
