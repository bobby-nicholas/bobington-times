# Science & Discovery

This file tracks scientific institutions, discoveries, natural phenomena, and technological advancements in Bobington's world.

## Institutions

- **The Royal Institute of Natural Philosophy** — Bobington's premier scientific body, founded 1743. Located on Arundel Crescent. Hosts public lectures on Thursday evenings. Current President: Professor Elara Whitstone (geologist, volcanologist).
- **Bobington Polytechnic** — The city's engineering and applied sciences college. Strong in electrical engineering and materials science. Dean: Dr. Oswald Fenn.
- **The Cartwright Observatory** — Hilltop observatory in the Marches district, built 1881. Houses the 36-inch Cartwright Refractor. Director: Dr. Sable Nightingale (astronomer).

## Current Scientific Threads

- **Greymoor Highland Luminous Phenomenon** — Persistent diffuse glow above the Greymoor Highlands, first documented by Dr. Odette Collis on 3 Feb 2026. Visible on clear nights (10 PM–2 AM), greenish-white, ~2° band above ridge. Through telescope: faint vertical striations. Spectral signature doesn't match known industrial or atmospheric sources. Coincides roughly in location/time with seismic tremor (23 Feb). Observatory has formally logged it and requested Royal Institute spectroscopic equipment time. See people.md: Dr. Odette Collis.
- **Underground Waterway Survey** — Municipal Works Office comprehensive survey of subsurface hydrology (commissioned by Kinnear, 6 months before Feb 2026). Surveyor Pella Strand discovered a substantial unmapped buried waterway beneath the Docklands — brick-lined, arched, ~2m wide, predates Victorian drainage. ~400m traced. Investigation ongoing through mid-March.
- **Calloway Comet (CONFIRMED)** — Edith Calloway, 73, amateur astronomer (14 Harbinger Lane, Bramblegate), discovered a new comet on Tue 17 Feb 2026 at 9:47 PM, in a star grouping she calls "the Fisherman's Basket." Cartwright Observatory (Dr. Sable Nightingale) confirmed "an object of interest" using the 36-inch refractor. **Ashford Republic's Southern Observatory independently confirmed the object on 25 Feb.** Thessarine Astronomical Society has not responded (political climate). Third request sent to Verlaine National Observatory. First comet discovered from Bobington in 41 years (since Merrifield, 1985), first by a non-credentialed observer. International Astronomical Registry (Fenmouth) expected to assign formal designation within 4-6 weeks. Royal Institute invited Mrs. Calloway to present at Thursday evening lecture series — first non-credentialed speaker in 14 years. **Calloway presentation (27 Feb, Thu evening):** Standing-room audience of 140+ in Meridian Lecture Room (largest Thu lecture since Whitstone's 2019 seismology series). Mrs Calloway spoke for 42 minutes, demonstrated her systematic scanning method, showed observation notebooks on document camera. Standing ovation lasting ~90 seconds. Nightingale: notebooks are "a treasure of amateur science." Fenmouth designation now considered a formality (2 independent confirmations in hand). Calloway went home afterwards to observe — sky was clear.

## Natural Phenomena

- **Greymoor Highland Luminous Phenomenon** — See Current Scientific Threads above. Greenish-white glow, vertical striations, visible since 3 Feb 2026. 23+ consecutive clear nights observed. Royal Institute granted portable spectrometer access (25 Feb). **First spectroscopic results (26 Feb):** Dr Collis and Dr Maren Ilkley (Royal Institute spectroscopist, age 30) operated portable spectrometer from ridge above Dunvale, Wed night 26 Feb, 10:15 PM-2:30 AM. Emission spectrum shows strong lines consistent with ionised nitrogen and trace sulphur dioxide. NOT typical atmospheric phenomenon. Consistent with volcanic/geothermal venting — superheated gases escaping through fractured rock. Whitstone: "The emission profile suggests a subsurface thermal source. Something beneath the Greymoor ridge is venting gas through the rock." Glow predates tremor by 3 weeks — both may be symptoms of same geothermal system. Complicates Greymoor mining expansion — geothermal activity adds new variable to geological survey. **Second observation (27 Feb, Thu night):** Collis and Ilkley operated from three successive positions along ridge. Glow extends along roughly 3km stretch of ridgeline (NNE-SSW). At least four discrete emission points identified. Same spectral signature at all stations — single connected geothermal process, not separate phenomena. Brightest emission near centre. Whitstone: "This is not a single vent. It is a system." Mapped extent runs through the zones containing most promising deep copper deposits. Royal Institute convening emergency geology/geophysics meeting early next week. Whitstone: recommended geological survey (1.2M florins) now "urgent," must include geothermal assessment. Considering permanent monitoring station. Collis: "The ridge is alive." **RI Emergency Panel (announced 28 Feb, meets Tue 3 Mar):** 6-member panel chaired by Whitstone. Members: Collis, Ilkley, seismologist from Bobington Polytechnic, mining geologist, Haversten (Miners' Cooperative). Mandate: define scope, methodology, and timeline of geological/geothermal survey. Also considering permanent monitoring station: 180,000 florins construction, 45,000/yr operation (first sustained scientific presence on Greymoor since 1950s met outpost abandoned). Commission interim report (28 Feb) formally mandates the survey. Panel recommendations will feed into commission final report (5 Mar).

## Harbour Seal Colony — Lower Ashwater (new, 13 Mar 2026)
- Colony of 7-9 harbour seals (*Phoca vitulina*) on mudflats below Bramblegate Steps. First recorded colony in lower Ashwater since 1891 (naturalist Clement Birch documented "a company of harbour seals, eight or ten in number" that year). Industrial discharge drove seals out of the river in early 20th century.
- Spotted by retired harbour pilot Reg Garside (74) on Tue 11 Mar. Confirmed by Dr Annalise Fenn-Coulthard (BINS) on Thu 13 Mar. Adults + at least 2 juveniles — not transient, establishing territory.
- Possible factors: improved water quality over past two decades; Lower Conduit outfall below Bramblegate Steps wharf creating cooler, fresher micro-habitat on the mudflats.
- Fenn-Coulthard has requested Harbour Authority designate mudflat as temporary wildlife protection zone and that ferry reduce speed past Bramblegate Steps at low tide. Ashby: "under consideration."

## Technology

The world is at roughly an early-to-mid 20th century technological level:
- Electric tramways (Bobington's copper-wire system)
- Motorcar and horse coexistence
- Early radio broadcasting
- Telegraph well-established
- Gas and electric lighting in transition
- No television, no computing, no aviation beyond experimental

## Greymoor Monitoring Station (updated 7 Mar)
- Approved: 195,000 florins construction + 48,000/yr operating. First permanent scientific presence on ridge since 1950s met outpost.
- **Equipment tenders issued 7 Mar:** 3 broadband seismometers (continuous recording to 15m depth), 2 atmospheric gas sampling units (SO2, H2S, CO2 at ppm sensitivity), meteorological mast (anemometer, barometer, temp at 3 heights), photometric array for continuous glow measurement, battery banks (48hr autonomous), insulated wind-rated shelter.
- Site: ~800m NW of old met outpost, above central emission zone. Accessible by pack track from Dunvale road.
- Tenders close 21 Mar. Construction Apr. Station operational mid-May for deep geological survey (250m depth, 1.65M florins).
- **Collis brightening (7 Mar):** 32nd consecutive night of observation. Reports "marginal but consistent" increase in brightness at central emission point. Photometric readings over past week show trend "difficult to attribute to atmospheric variation." Not dramatic — "not a flare, not a surge." Spectral signature unchanged (ionised nitrogen + SO2). Significance unclear pending instrumental monitoring.
- **Collis/Ilkley presentation (10 Mar, Tue):** Preliminary photometric data presented to ~60 Fellows at Royal Institute Meridian Lecture Room. Brightening confirmed at 12-15% since first observation (3 Feb). Rate of increase gradual but consistent, with no night of reversal. 36th consecutive night. "Beyond plausible atmospheric variation." Spectral signature unchanged. Ilkley: "The same process, intensifying. The source is stable but growing." Whitstone: geothermal component of geological survey now "essential." Haversten attended, in conversation with Whitstone afterwards.
