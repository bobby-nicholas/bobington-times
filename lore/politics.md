# Politics & Government

## City Government
- **Mayor:** Harriet Blackthorne — supportive of tramway, presenting Freedom of the City to Rovers
- **City Council** — Voted 7-4 to approve Veridan Corridor Tramway Expansion
- **Council Speaker:** Desmond Falk
- **Key council members:** Ida Pryce (Docklands, pro-tramway), Aldric Voss (anti-tramway, fiscal conservative)
- **Municipal Chamber** — City council chamber, marble halls

## Political Dynamics
- Council split between modernisers (Pryce faction, pro-infrastructure) and fiscal conservatives (Voss faction, prioritise schools and emergency services)
- Tramway vote was 7-4, suggesting the modernisers hold a comfortable majority
- Blackthorne aligned with the modernisers

## Law Enforcement
- **Metropolitan Constabulary** — Police force. Investigating Greystone Wharf arson.
- **Fire Marshal's Office** — Edwin Hale leading fire investigations

## Bobington Foreign Office
- Issued restrained statement on Kaelmar Strait tensions urging dialogue (15 Feb)
- Dispatched envoy Sir Duncan Hale to Thessara for consultations (17 Feb)

## Copper Review Commission
- Established 19 Feb by unanimous council vote (11-0)
- Co-chaired by Councilwoman Ida Pryce and Councilman Aldric Voss
- Mandate: assess tramway options (proceed/redesign/phase) and report by 5 March
- First meeting: Monday 24 Feb. Okonkwo, Treasury, copper suppliers to testify.

## Docklands Safety Audit
- Authorised 19 Feb (8-3 vote, Voss and 2 allies opposed on procedural grounds)
- Comprehensive audit of all vacant commercial properties in the Docklands
- Focus: fire safety, insurance compliance, security arrangements
- Proposed by Pryce in the wake of the Greystone Wharf arson

## Political Dynamics (updated)
- Council split tested by copper crisis; forced rare bipartisan cooperation (Pryce-Voss commission)
- Speaker Falk described the pairing as "either inspired or catastrophic"
- Blackthorne navigating between factions; struck tone of "measured resolve"
- Docklands Workers' Association (foreman Patrick Seldon) emerging as new political voice — seeking formal meeting with council re: workforce protections
